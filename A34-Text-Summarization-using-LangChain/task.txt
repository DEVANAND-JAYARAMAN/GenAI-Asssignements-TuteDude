1. What is Stuff Chain?
Stuff chain combines the entire document into one prompt and sends it to the LLM for summarization

2. When is it suitable?
It is suitable for short documents that fit within the model's context window

3. Limitations
Cannot handle very large documents, may exceed token limits, and it is less effective for lengthy inputs

Task 14 from the Assignment:

1. Best strategy for very long documents

Map-Reduce is generally the best choice because it efficiently processes large documents by splitting them into chunks and then combining their summaries

2. Trade-offs between speed and quality: 
Prompt-based: Fastest but limited to short inputs
Stuff: Fast but constrained by the model's context window
Map-Reduce: Balanced speed and quality for long documents
Refine: Highest coherence and quality but slower due to sequential processing

3. Real-world use cases
Prompt-based: Summarizing emails, short articles, or notes
Stuff: Reports or documents that fit within the context window
Map-Reduce: Research papers, books, meeting transcripts, and lengthy PDFs
